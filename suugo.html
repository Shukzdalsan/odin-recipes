Suugo Suqaar (Somali Pasta Sauce) Recipe

Description

Suugo Suqaar is a flavorful Somali pasta sauce typically made with ground beef or lamb, tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic spices. This rich and savory sauce is traditionally served over spaghetti, reflecting Italian influences on Somali cuisine. The preparation involves sautéing onions, garlic, and green bell peppers, adding meat, and simmering with tomatoes and tomato paste until the sauce thickens. Spices such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric infuse the dish with a warm and robust flavor, making Suugo Suqaar a comforting and hearty meal often garnished with fresh cilantro and enjoyed by families across Somalia.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef or lamb
  • onions
  • garlic
  • green bell pepper
  • tomatoes
  • tomato paste
  • cumin
  • coriander
  • turmeric
  • black pepper
  • salt
  • oil
  • water
  • spaghetti
  • fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil water in a large pot, add a pinch of salt, and cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: In a large pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and cook until they are translucent. Add minced garlic and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add Meat: Add ground beef or lamb to the pan and cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  4. Incorporate Vegetables: Add chopped green bell pepper to the meat mixture and cook for a few minutes until softened.
  5. Add Tomatoes and Paste: Stir in chopped tomatoes and tomato paste, cooking until the tomatoes break down and the mixture thickens.
  6. Season the Sauce: Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, black pepper, and salt. Stir well to combine the spices with the sauce.
  7. Simmer: Pour in a bit of water to reach your desired sauce consistency. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  8. Serve: Toss the cooked spaghetti with the sauce. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
