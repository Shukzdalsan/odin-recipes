Bariis Recipe

Description

Bariis is a traditional Somali rice dish that is known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices. Typically made with basmati rice, Bariis combines a delightful mix of ingredients that result in a savory and slightly sweet taste. The preparation starts with sautéing onions and garlic in ghee or oil until they are golden brown, infusing the base with a deep, caramelized flavor. Spices such as cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, and cumin are then added, creating a fragrant and warm profile that is characteristic of Somali cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • ghee or oil
  • cinnamon
  • cloves
  • cardamom
  • cumin
  • tomatoes
  • water or broth
  • raisins or currants (optional)
  • peas (optional)
  • carrots (optional)
  • meat (goat, chicken, or beef - optional)
  • fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • fried onions (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Rinse the Rice: Thoroughly rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: In a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and cook until they are golden brown. Add minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add Spices: Stir in the cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom pods, and cumin seeds. Cook for a few minutes until the spices release their aroma.
  4. Incorporate Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook until they soften and blend with the onions and spices, creating a thick mixture.
  5. Cook the Rice: Add the drained rice to the pot, stirring to coat the grains with the spice mixture. Pour in water or broth (typically in a ratio of 1.5 to 2 parts liquid to 1 part rice). Season with salt to taste.
  6. Add Optional Ingredients: If using raisins or currants, peas, or carrots, add them to the pot at this stage. Stir gently to combine.
  7. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Prepare the Meat (if using): While the rice is cooking, prepare the meat separately. Season the meat with spices that complement the rice (such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric) and cook it until tender.
  9. Rest the Rice: Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  10. Garnish and Serve: Fluff the rice with a fork and transfer it to a serving platter. Garnish with fresh cilantro and fried onions. Serve with the prepared meat on the side, if desired.
